# Glossary

Key terms and concepts used throughout OpenZEV.

## A

**Active Participant**
A participant whose **Valid To** date is in the future (or blank), meaning they are currently a member of the ZEV. See [Managing Participants](03-participant-management.md).

**Admin**
System administrator role with unrestricted access to all ZEVs, settings, and user accounts. See [Roles and Permissions](11-roles-and-permissions.md).

**Allocation**
Distribution of community energy production among participants proportional to their consumption at each timestamp. See [How Energy Allocation Works](08-billing-allocation-explained.md).

## B

**Billing Interval**
Frequency at which invoices are generated: monthly, quarterly, semi-annual, or annual. Set in [ZEV Settings](02-zev-setup.md#billing-interval).

**Bidirectional Meter**
A metering point that measures both consumption (IN) and production (OUT). Common for homes with solar panels and grid consumption.

## C

**Community**
See **ZEV**.

**Consumption**
Energy drawn from the grid or ZEV production. Measured by consumption meters (type `IN`).

**Coverage**
Percentage of expected metering readings received during a period. 100% coverage means all hourly readings are present. See [Metering Analysis](06-metering-analysis.md).

## D

**Data Quality**
Assessment of metering data completeness and health. Green (complete), yellow (partial), red (missing). See [Metering Analysis](06-metering-analysis.md).

**Draft Invoice**
Invoice in initial state, not yet approved. Can be edited or regenerated. See [Invoice Management](09-invoice-management.md).

## E

**Energy Tariff**
Price per kWh charged for energy consumed (local, grid, or feed-in). See [Tariff Configuration](07-tariff-configuration.md).

**Export / ExportedEnergy**
Energy produced by a participant and fed back to the community/grid. Also called **Feed-In**.

## F

**Feed-In**
Energy produced by a participant (e.g., from solar panels) that is fed back to the community or external grid. Typically credited at a lower price than local energy. See [Tariff Configuration](07-tariff-configuration.md).

**Feed-In Tariff**
Price per kWh credited for participant production fed back.

**Fixed Fee**
Flat monthly, quarterly, or annual charge (not energy-based). Examples: community admin fee, meter maintenance fee. See [Tariff Configuration](07-tariff-configuration.md).

**Flat Rate**
Constant price per kWh throughout the day/period. Contrast with HT/NT (time-of-use).

## G

**Gap**
Missing metering readings during an expected period. Identified in [Data Quality](06-metering-analysis.md) analysis.

**Grid Energy**
Energy consumed from the external grid (not available from ZEV production). Typically more expensive than local energy. See [Billing](08-billing-allocation-explained.md).

**Grid Tariff**
Price per kWh charged for grid energy consumed.

## H

**HT (High Tariff)**
Higher price per kWh during peak hours (e.g., 06:00–22:00). Used in time-of-use tariffs. Contrast with **NT**.

## I

**Import**
Process of uploading metering data (CSV, Excel, SDAT-CH) into OpenZEV. See [Metering Import](05-metering-import.md).

**Invoice**
Bill generated for a participant covering a billing period. Contains energy charges and fixed fees. See [Invoice Management](09-invoice-management.md).

**Invoicing Period**
Time range covered by an invoice (e.g., January 1–31, Q1 Jan–Mar, etc.).

## L

**Local Energy**
Energy consumed by a participant that is supplied from the ZEV's own production (not the external grid). Typically cheaper than grid energy. See [Billing](08-billing-allocation-explained.md).

**Local Pool**
Total energy available within the ZEV at a given timestamp = min(production, consumption). See [Billing](08-billing-allocation-explained.md).

**Local Tariff**
Price per kWh charged for local energy consumed.

## M

**Metering Point**
Physical or logical energy meter owned by a participant. Measures consumption, production, or both. Identified by unique ID (MSID). See [Metering Points](04-metering-points.md).

**MSID**
Meter Serial ID or Metering Point ID — unique identifier for a physical meter.

## N

**NT (Night Tariff)**
Lower price per kWh during off-peak hours (e.g., 22:00–06:00). Used in time-of-use tariffs. Contrast with **HT**.

## P

**Participant**
Member of a ZEV community with metering points and energy consumption/production. See [Managing Participants](03-participant-management.md).

**Period**
Time window for billing or analysis (day, week, month, quarter, year).

**Production**
Energy generated by a participant (e.g., solar, wind) and fed back to the community/grid. Measured by production meters (type `OUT`).

**Production Meter**
See **Metering Point**, type `OUT`.

## R

**Reading**
Single-point measurement of energy consumption or production at a specific timestamp. Unit: kWh.

**Resolution**
Granularity of metering readings: hourly (1 reading per hour), daily (1 per day), or monthly (1 per month). See [Metering Points](04-metering-points.md).

## S

**SDAT-CH**
Swiss standard for metering data exchange. OpenZEV supports SDAT-CH uploads. See [Metering Import](05-metering-import.md).

**Self-Consumption / Self-Sufficiency**
The fraction of local energy vs. total consumption. Example: 60 kWh local ÷ 100 kWh total = 60% self-consumption.

**Sent Invoice**
Invoice delivered to participant (email sent). Status: `Sent`. See [Invoice Management](09-invoice-management.md).

## T

**Tariff**
Pricing rule for energy or fixed fees. Can be energy tariff (per kWh) or fixed fee (monthly/yearly). See [Tariff Configuration](07-tariff-configuration.md).

**Tariff Period**
Time-of-day subdivision of a tariff (e.g., HT 06:00–22:00, NT 22:00–06:00). See [Tariff Configuration](07-tariff-configuration.md).

**Timestamp**
Specific point in time when a reading was recorded (e.g., 2026-01-15 14:00:00).

**Timezone**
Time zone used for interpreting timestamps during import. Critical for accurate billing alignment. See [Metering Import](05-metering-import.md#timezone-handling).

## V

**VAT**
Value Added Tax. In Switzerland, 8.1% (standard rate). OpenZEV applies VAT if ZEV has a VAT number. See [Admin Settings](02-zev-setup.md#vat-configuration).

**Validity Period**
Date range during which a participant or a participant-meter assignment is active. Defined by **Valid From** and **Valid To** dates.

## Z

**ZEV / vZEV**
Virtuelle Eigenverbrauchsgemeinschaft (Virtual Self-Consumption Community) in Swiss law. An energy community where members share local production. See [ZEV Setup](02-zev-setup.md).

**ZEV Scope**
Data isolation boundary for a ZEV. A ZEV Owner can only access their assigned ZEV(s). Participants see only their own data within their ZEV.

## Related Concepts

### Billing Model

OpenZEV uses a **timestamp-level allocation model**:
1. At each moment, determine how much local energy is available
2. Allocate proportionally to consumption
3. Remaining consumption is billed at grid rate
4. Sum over billing period
5. Apply tariffs and fixed fees

See [How Energy Allocation Works](08-billing-allocation-explained.md) for full explanation.

### Data Privacy

- **Participants** see only their own data
- **ZEV Owners** see only their assigned ZEV's data
- **Admins** can see all data
- Data is encrypted at rest and in transit

See [Roles and Permissions](11-roles-and-permissions.md).

### Audit Trail

All actions (login, invoice generation, email send) are logged with:
- User (who did it)
- Action (what was done)
- Timestamp (when)
- Changes (old → new values)

Available to admins in audit logs.
